# Marketing Plan
## Positioning
### Primary Positioning Statement
"AiPrompter: The AI-powered prompt crafting platform that boosts LLM performance by 30% in just 2 weeks."

### Alternative Positioning Statements
1. "PromptCraft: The SaaS solution for data scientists and developers to create, test, and refine LLM prompts with ease."
2. "PromptGenie: The AI-driven platform that automates prompt crafting, saving you 50% of development time."
3. "PromptPro: The expert-level tool for crafting high-quality LLM prompts, ensuring 25% better model performance."

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
### Persona 1: Emma, Data Scientist
- Daily Job: Developing and deploying LLM models for a Fortune 500 company
- Budget: $10,000 - $50,000 per year for AI-related tools and services
- Pain Points: Difficulty in crafting effective LLM prompts, limited time for testing and refinement

### Persona 2: Ryan, AI Engineer
- Daily Job: Building and maintaining large-scale AI systems for a tech startup
- Budget: $5,000 - $20,000 per year for AI-related tools and services
- Pain Points: Struggling to optimize LLM performance, limited resources for prompt development

### Persona 3: Dr. Patel, Research Scientist
- Daily Job: Conducting research on natural language processing and AI applications
- Budget: $20,000 - $100,000 per year for research-related tools and services
- Pain Points: Need for efficient and effective LLM prompt development, limited time for testing and refinement

## Channels
### Top 3 Channels with CAC Estimate
1. **Content Marketing** (CAC: $10)
	* Blog posts, case studies, and whitepapers on LLM prompt crafting and optimization
	* Target audience: Data scientists, AI engineers, and research scientists
2. **Social Media** (CAC: $15)
	* LinkedIn, Twitter, and Reddit advertising targeting ICP personas
	* Content: Promotional posts, engaging discussions, and thought leadership pieces
3. **Email Marketing** (CAC: $20)
	* Targeted email campaigns to ICP personas with relevant content and promotions
	* Content: Exclusive resources, webinars, and product updates

## Content Cadence
### Week-by-Week Content Plan
Week 1-2: Introduction to LLM prompt crafting and optimization
Week 3-4: Case studies and success stories
Week 5-6: Technical guides and tutorials
Week 7-8: Webinars and workshops
Week 9-10: Product updates and feature announcements
Week 11-12: User-generated content and community engagement

## Launch Milestones
### D-30 (30 days before launch)
- Finalize product development and testing
- Establish content calendar and social media presence
- Launch email marketing campaigns

### D-0 (launch day)
- Launch product and marketing campaigns
- Host webinars and workshops
- Share launch announcements on social media

### D+30 (30 days after launch)
- Analyze launch metrics and adjust marketing strategies
- Introduce new content formats (e.g., videos, podcasts)
- Host user-generated content contests

### D+90 (90 days after launch)
- Evaluate success metrics and adjust product roadmap
- Introduce new features and updates
- Host a user conference or meetup

## Success Metrics
### Daily Active Users (DAU)
- Target: 100 DAU within the first 30 days
- Metric: Track DAU growth and adjust marketing strategies accordingly

### $MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue) Target
- Target: $10,000 MRR within the first 90 days
- Metric: Track MRR growth and adjust pricing strategies accordingly
